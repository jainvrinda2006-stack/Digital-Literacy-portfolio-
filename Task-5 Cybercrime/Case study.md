# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud has become one of the most common cybercrimes, especially in India, due to the rapid growth of digital payments. In a typical case, a fraudster contacts the victim through a phone call, message, or online marketplace pretending to be a buyer, bank official, or customer support agent. First, they gain the victim’s trust by sounding convincing and creating urgency. Next, they send a “payment request” or a fake link and instruct the victim to approve it, claiming it is needed to receive money. Many people mistakenly believe they are receiving money, but instead, they end up authorizing a payment.

Such frauds usually target students, elderly people, or individuals who are not fully aware of how digital payment systems work. The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online platforms. In some cases, victims may lose large amounts of money within minutes.

This situation can be avoided by following basic precautions such as never sharing OTPs or PINs, carefully reading payment requests, and verifying the identity of unknown callers. Awareness and cautious behavior are the most effective ways to prevent such cybercrimes.
